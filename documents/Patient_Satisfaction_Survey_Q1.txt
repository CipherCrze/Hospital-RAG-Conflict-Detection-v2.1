PATIENT SATISFACTION SURVEY — Q1 2025 RESULTS REPORT
Survey Period: January 2025 — March 2025
Sample Size: 840 respondents across inpatient and outpatient services
Administered by: Quality and Patient Experience Department
Report Date: 07 April 2025
Classification: Internal

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Q1 2025 Patient Satisfaction Survey shows a broadly positive picture of
patient experience across the hospital. The overall Patient Satisfaction Index
(PSI) for Q1 2025 is 78.4 (out of 100), compared to a Q4 2024 PSI of 65.3.
This represents an improvement of 20.1% — the largest single-quarter
improvement since the PSI instrument was introduced in 2021.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DOMAIN SCORES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Overall Communication (Doctor–Patient): 84.1 / 100 (Q4: 79.2) ↑ 6.2%
Nursing Care Quality:                   81.7 / 100 (Q4: 78.0) ↑ 4.7%
Cleanliness and Hygiene:               89.2 / 100 (Q4: 82.6) ↑ 8.0%
Waiting Time Experience:               62.3 / 100 (Q4: 59.8) ↑ 4.2%
Food and Facilities:                   73.5 / 100 (Q4: 70.1) ↑ 4.9%
Discharge Process:                     77.8 / 100 (Q4: 74.3) ↑ 4.7%

Patient satisfaction trended upward through Q1 with improvements observed
across all six measured domains. The surge in the overall PSI is partly
attributable to a revised weighting methodology introduced in January 2025
that gives greater weight to communication and nursing care scores.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT SPECIFIC FINDINGS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Emergency Department satisfaction subscore for Q1 2025 is 71.2 / 100,
an improvement from the Q4 2024 subscore of 63.8 / 100 (a rise of 11.6%).
Respondents in the ED category rated nursing attentiveness and communication
positively, with 68% rating their ED experience as "Good" or "Excellent".

Note: ED satisfaction data was collected primarily in January and early
February. Survey administration in the ED was paused from mid-February due
to staffing pressures; the ED subscore therefore reflects a truncated sample
and may not fully represent the Q1 experience for ED patients.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METHODOLOGY NOTE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The PSI improvement from 65.3 to 78.4 reflects both genuine improvements in
patient care and the change in weighting methodology. Under the previous
(Q4 2024) weighting, the Q1 score would be approximately 69.8, representing
a more modest improvement of approximately 7%. The Finance Department's
reference to a 20% improvement in satisfaction in the Q1 Financial Summary
corresponds to the new weighting methodology and is directionally correct.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LIMITATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This survey captures self-reported satisfaction and does not capture
complaints formally lodged through the Patient Relations Department. The
Patient Complaints Register should be reviewed alongside this report for
a complete picture of patient experience in Q1 2025.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RECOMMENDATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Restore ED survey administration in Q2, with particular focus on patients
   presenting in February and March when staffing pressures were highest.
2. Investigate the gap between high survey satisfaction scores and the
   significant increase in formal complaints recorded in Q1 (see Complaints
   Register for details).
