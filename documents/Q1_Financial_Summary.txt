HOSPITAL ADMINISTRATIVE SERVICES
Q1 FINANCIAL SUMMARY REPORT — JANUARY TO MARCH 2025
Prepared by: Department of Finance
Date: 10 April 2025
Classification: Internal

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXECUTIVE OVERVIEW
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Finance Department is pleased to report that the hospital closed Q1 2025
within budgetary parameters across all major cost centres. Total Q1 operational
expenditure was INR 14.82 crore against an approved budget of INR 15.50 crore,
representing a favourable variance of INR 0.68 crore (4.4% underspend).

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Emergency Department's Q1 budget allocation was INR 3.20 crore. Actual
expenditure recorded for the quarter was INR 3.01 crore, giving a favourable
variance of INR 0.19 crore. ED budget utilisation stood at 94.1%, well within
acceptable thresholds.

Procurement costs for ED consumables were INR 0.82 crore against a budget of
INR 0.90 crore. Personnel costs including overtime were INR 1.47 crore, which
was INR 0.08 crore below budget due to some shift vacancies in February.

The Finance Department confirms that no emergency procurement requests from ED
were processed during Q1, indicating that supply chain needs were met through
regular procurement cycles.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
NURSING SERVICES COST CENTRE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Total nursing services expenditure for Q1 was INR 4.10 crore against a budget
of INR 4.25 crore. Staff overtime payments totalled INR 0.31 crore. The
Nursing cost centre recorded a favourable variance of INR 0.15 crore.

Agency nurse deployments during Q1 were minimal — 14 agency shifts totalling
approximately 112 agency hours — contributing INR 0.09 crore to the overtime
line, which was accounted for within the existing budget envelope.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PATIENT SATISFACTION PROGRAMME — BUDGETARY NOTE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Patient Experience Programme budget of INR 0.22 crore was fully utilised
during Q1. Survey administration, data collection, and reporting activities
proceeded as planned. Based on preliminary satisfaction index data shared by
the Quality Department, the programme is delivering improved outcomes.
Overall patient satisfaction improved by 20% compared to Q4 2024 benchmark.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CAPITAL EXPENDITURE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No major capital expenditure was recorded in Q1. Medical equipment maintenance
contracts were renewed within budget. The planned CT scanner upgrade is
deferred to Q2 pending vendor finalisation.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OUTLOOK
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Finance Department projects continued budgetary compliance through Q2 2025,
assuming no unplanned procurement requirements. The ED department is operating
efficiently within its resource envelope and no supplementary budget allocation
is anticipated.

Prepared by: Chief Financial Officer, Hospital Administrative Services
Reviewed by: Hospital Superintendent
