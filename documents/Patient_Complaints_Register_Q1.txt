PATIENT COMPLAINTS REGISTER — Q1 2025 ANALYSIS REPORT
Period Covered: 01 January 2025 — 31 March 2025
Prepared by: Patient Relations Department
Report Date: 09 April 2025
Classification: Internal — Complaints and Grievances
Total Complaints Received in Q1 2025: 312

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OVERVIEW
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Patient Relations Department received 312 formal complaints during Q1 2025.
This represents a 25% increase over Q4 2024 (249 complaints) and is the
highest quarterly complaint volume since Q1 2023. The increase is concentrated
in two categories: Emergency Department wait times and nursing staff availability.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT COMPLAINTS — DETAILED ANALYSIS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Emergency Department accounted for 89 wait-time complaints in Q1 2025,
more than double the Q4 2024 figure of 41. This sharp rise is directly
correlated with the deterioration in ED operational conditions from February
2025 onwards (extended nurse leave, agency staff deployment, supply shortages).

Representative complaint excerpts from the ED category:
"We waited 55 minutes before anyone spoke to us. The department looked
overwhelmed and understaffed." — Patient, February 2025

"I understand hospitals are busy but the triage nurse told us they were
short-staffed that day. A patient next to us left without being seen."
— Family member, March 2025

"Wait times seem to have gotten significantly worse compared to our visit
in December. Not sure why." — Returning patient, March 2025

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
NURSING AVAILABILITY COMPLAINTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Nursing availability complaints (67 in Q1) are the second-largest driver of
the overall increase. These complaints are concentrated in February and March
and specifically relate to response times to call-button activations and
delays in medication administration due to high workload.

ED complaint volume rose by 25% in Q1 compared to Q4. The pattern is
inconsistent with the Patient Satisfaction Survey report which indicates an
overall satisfaction improvement of 20% for the same period. The Patient
Relations Department notes that formal complaint registration and
satisfaction survey responses may capture different segments of patient
experience, but the magnitude of the discrepancy warrants investigation.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
POSITIVE OBSERVATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Complaints related to cleanliness and hygiene decreased by 37%, reflecting the
effectiveness of the infection control programme. Food and facilities complaints
also declined. These positive signals are consistent with the satisfaction
survey findings in those domains.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RESOLUTION STATUS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Of the 312 complaints received:
- 287 (92%) were formally acknowledged within 72 hours (SLA target: 90%)
- 241 (77%) were resolved and closed within 30 days (SLA target: 75%)
- 71 (23%) remain under investigation or pending resolution at report date

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RECOMMENDATIONS TO HOSPITAL BOARD
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Commission a root-cause investigation into the February–March ED complaint
   surge, with specific focus on staffing levels and supply chain readiness.
2. Review the disconnect between satisfaction survey scores and formal
   complaint data to ensure measurement instruments are complementary.
3. Implement mandatory complaint trend reporting to the Hospital Board on a
   monthly basis (currently quarterly).
