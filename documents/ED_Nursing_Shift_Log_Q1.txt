EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT — NURSING SHIFT LOG
Q1 2025 CONSOLIDATED SUPERVISOR REPORT
January — March 2025
Prepared by: ED Head Nurse / Shift Supervisor
Department: Emergency Department
Classification: Internal — Department Records

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OPERATIONAL SUMMARY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This report consolidates shift supervisor observations across all Emergency
Department nursing shifts during Q1 2025 (January 1 to March 31). It is
intended to provide the Hospital Superintendent with a ground-level view of
operational pressures not fully captured in departmental financial reporting.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STAFFING AND BUDGET PRESSURES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
January 2025:
Shift rosters were met adequately in January. Staffing levels were close to
planned. Minor supply replenishment delays were noted but resolved within SLA.

February 2025:
Significant staffing pressure developed from the second week of February.
Three senior nursing staff were on extended medical leave simultaneously.
Agency staff were brought in for 22 shifts — substantially above the annual
average. The ED operated at a nurse-to-patient ratio of 1:6 on 11 separate
shifts, which is above the safe maximum of 1:4.5 per hospital policy.

The ED nursing supervisor formally submitted two emergency procurement requests
to the Materials Management Department in February — one for IV cannulas and
one for sterile dressing kits — as regular stock levels fell critically short.
Both requests were fulfilled within 48 hours but confirm that routine
procurement was insufficient to meet Q1 demand.

March 2025:
Supply shortages continued into March for certain high-use consumables
(disposable gloves, oxygen masks). Three more emergency procurement requests
were submitted. Staff overtime in March was the highest recorded since Q3 2022.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXPENDITURE OBSERVATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emergency Department spending exceeded allocation by approximately 12% when
agency nurse costs and emergency procurement surcharges are included on a
cash-accrual basis. The discrepancy with the Finance Department's reported
favourable variance arises from the use of accrual accounting in the Finance
report versus cash-basis expenditure tracking in departmental records.

Total agency nursing hours in Q1: 352 hours (budget: 112 hours)
Emergency procurement requests raised: 5 (budget assumption: 0)
Overtime hours: 1,480 (budget: 820)

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PATIENT EXPERIENCE IMPACT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The staffing pressures described above had a direct impact on patient waiting
times and experience in the ED. Average ED wait times increased from 24 minutes
(Q4 2024 baseline) to 41 minutes by March 2025. Several patients left without
being seen (LWBS rate: 4.2%) — above the hospital's target of 2.0%.

Patient complaint volumes directed at the ED nursing team rose sharply in
February and March. This is consistent with the Patient Complaints Register
data and is a direct consequence of under-staffing, not of nursing quality.
Patient satisfaction in the ED showed a consistent decline following the
February staffing changes.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RECOMMENDATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Review ED staffing budget for Q2 to reflect actual agency and overtime costs.
2. Increase safety stock levels for high-use consumables to avoid emergency
   procurement scenarios.
3. Investigate discrepancy between Finance Department budget figures and ED
   departmental expenditure records.
