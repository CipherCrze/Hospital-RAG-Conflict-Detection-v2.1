OUTPATIENT SERVICES — PATIENT FEEDBACK AGGREGATION REPORT

Prepared by: Outpatient Department Administration
Date: 09 April 2025
Classification: Internal

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OVERVIEW
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This report aggregates feedback collected via paper-based feedback forms
distributed at the Outpatient Department (OPD) exit points during Q1 2025.
A total of 1,247 feedback forms were collected and analysed. The OPD feedback
instrument differs from the Quality Department's Patient Satisfaction Survey
in both methodology and patient population, and results should not be compared
directly with PSI scores without adjustment.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TREND ANALYSIS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Overall OPD satisfaction shows a modest improvement of 2.6% compared to Q4
2024. The improvement is narrow and within the statistical margin of error
for this sample size. Declining satisfaction in diagnostic wait times
(laboratory and radiology) is a concern and warrants investigation.

Registration and consultation experience improvements align with the recent
deployment of the digital token management system in January 2025. Pharmacy
wait times remain a known bottleneck, though the slight improvement suggests
incremental progress.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OPEN-ENDED FEEDBACK THEMES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Positive themes (frequency > 50 mentions):
- "Doctors are attentive and explain clearly" (182 mentions)
- "Registration process is faster than before" (134 mentions)
- "Cleaner than I remember" (98 mentions)
- "Nursing staff are helpful" (87 mentions)

Negative themes (frequency > 50 mentions):
- "Lab results take too long" (201 mentions — highest frequency)
- "Pharmacy queue is very long" (156 mentions)
- "Air conditioning not working in waiting area — Block B" (74 mentions)
- "Parking is inadequate" (63 mentions)

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT REFERRALS FROM OPD
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
18 OPD patients were referred to the Emergency Department during Q1 2025.
Of the 11 patients who provided feedback post-ED referral, 8 (72%) reported
an unsatisfactory experience in the ED, citing long wait times and insufficient
nursing staff. This finding is consistent with the ED complaint data for the
same period and with the nursing shift logs reporting understaffing in February
and March 2025.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COMPARISON WITH Q1 PATIENT SATISFACTION SURVEY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Q1 Patient Satisfaction Survey (Quality Department) reports a 20% overall
improvement in the hospital-wide PSI. The OPD feedback aggregation, which
uses a different instrument and primarily measures OPD-specific experience,
shows only a 2.6% improvement in overall OPD satisfaction. The large difference
is likely attributable to the change in PSI weighting methodology introduced
in January 2025 rather than an actual 20% improvement in patient experience.
Administrators should interpret the satisfaction survey's 20% headline figure
with caution and reference the methodology note in that report.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RECOMMENDATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Prioritise radiology and laboratory capacity assessment to address
   declining diagnostic wait time scores.
2. Escalate the Block B air-conditioning issue to Facilities Management.
3. Align OPD feedback instrument with the Quality Department's PSI methodology
   to enable meaningful cross-instrument comparison in future quarters.
4. Investigate the ED patient experience following OPD referrals — the 72%
   unsatisfactory rate in the ED referral cohort is a significant finding.
