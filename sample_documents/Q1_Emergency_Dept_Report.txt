===============================================
   CITYCARE GENERAL HOSPITAL
   Emergency Department — Q1 Performance Report
   Period: January – March 2025
===============================================

DEPARTMENT OVERVIEW
-------------------
The Emergency Department (ED) experienced a challenging Q1 2025, marked by a
significant increase in patient volume and growing dissatisfaction among
patients and families.

PATIENT VOLUME & WAIT TIMES
----------------------------
- Total ED visits: 14,820 (up 18% from Q4 2024's 12,560)
- Average wait time to be seen: 4 hours 12 minutes (up from 2 hours 45 minutes)
- Patients who left without being seen (LWBS): 8.3% (up from 4.1%)
- Average length of stay in ED: 6.8 hours (up from 5.1 hours)

COMPLAINTS & PATIENT FEEDBACK
------------------------------
Patient complaints in the Emergency Department rose by 25% compared to Q4 2024:
- Total formal complaints filed: 312 (up from 250)
- Top complaint categories:
  1. Excessive wait times (42% of complaints)
  2. Poor communication about delays (28%)
  3. Rude or dismissive staff (15%)
  4. Inadequate pain management (10%)
  5. Billing issues (5%)

Several patients reported being left in hallways on gurneys for extended
periods. Two formal grievances were escalated to the Patient Advocate office.

STAFFING CHALLENGES
-------------------
- The ED operated at 72% of recommended staffing levels during January
  and February due to seasonal illness among staff.
- Three senior ED physicians resigned in Q1, citing burnout.
- Nurse overtime hours increased by 35%.
- Agency/travel nurse utilization rose to 22% of total nursing hours.

CRITICAL INCIDENTS
------------------
- 3 sentinel events reported (compared to 1 in Q4 2024)
- 1 case of delayed diagnosis attributed to overcrowding
- 2 cases of medication errors during high-volume periods

QUALITY METRICS
---------------
- Door-to-doctor time: 78 minutes (target: 30 minutes)
- Left without being seen rate: 8.3% (benchmark: <2%)
- Patient satisfaction (ED-specific): 58% (down from 65%)
- 30-day ED revisit rate: 12.4% (up from 9.8%)

RECOMMENDATIONS
---------------
1. Immediate hiring of 5 additional ED physicians and 12 nurses
2. Implementation of fast-track system for low-acuity patients
3. Real-time wait time communication system for patients
4. Mandatory overtime caps to reduce staff burnout

Prepared by: Dr. James Chen, Emergency Department Director
Date: April 8, 2025
