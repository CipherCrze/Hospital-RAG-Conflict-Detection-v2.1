===============================================
   CITYCARE GENERAL HOSPITAL
   Q1 Patient Satisfaction Survey Results
   Period: January – March 2025
===============================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
Overall patient satisfaction at CityCarе General Hospital improved significantly
in Q1 2025. Based on 2,340 survey responses collected across all departments,
our composite satisfaction score rose from 72.1% (Q4 2024) to 86.5% (Q1 2025),
representing an improvement of approximately 20%.

KEY FINDINGS
------------
1. Communication with Physicians:
   - 89% of patients rated physician communication as "Good" or "Excellent,"
     up from 78% in Q4.
   - Notable improvements in explaining diagnoses and treatment options.

2. Nursing Care:
   - Nursing care satisfaction increased to 91%, the highest score in 3 years.
   - Patients specifically praised responsiveness and compassion.

3. Facility Cleanliness:
   - Cleanliness scores improved to 85% satisfaction, up from 79%.
   - New cleaning protocols implemented in January are showing results.

4. Discharge Process:
   - Discharge process satisfaction improved to 82%, up 11 points.
   - Patients reported better understanding of post-discharge instructions.

5. Wait Times (Inpatient):
   - Inpatient wait time satisfaction improved to 76%, up from 68%.
   - Average wait for scheduled procedures reduced by 22 minutes.

6. Overall Likelihood to Recommend:
   - Net Promoter Score (NPS) increased from 42 to 61.
   - 87% of patients said they would recommend CityCarе to family and friends.

DEPARTMENT BREAKDOWN
--------------------
- Cardiology:       92% satisfaction (up 15%)
- Orthopedics:      88% satisfaction (up 12%)
- General Medicine: 84% satisfaction (up 18%)
- Pediatrics:       90% satisfaction (up 10%)
- Emergency Dept:   73% satisfaction (up 8%)

METHODOLOGY
-----------
Surveys were distributed via email (65%), paper forms (25%), and phone (10%)
within 72 hours of discharge. Response rate: 34%, up from 28% in Q4.

CONCLUSION
----------
The Q1 results demonstrate a clear upward trend in patient satisfaction across
all departments. The hospital's Patient Experience Initiative, launched in
December 2024, appears to be driving these improvements. We recommend
continuing current strategies and focusing on areas still below 80%.
