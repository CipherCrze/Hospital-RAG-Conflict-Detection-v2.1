===============================================
   CITYCARE GENERAL HOSPITAL
   Human Resources — Staff Turnover Report
   Period: January – March 2025
===============================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
Staff turnover at CityCarе General Hospital increased significantly in Q1 2025.
The overall annualized turnover rate rose to 22.4%, up from 16.8% in Q4 2024 —
a 33% increase. This trend is concerning and requires immediate attention from
hospital leadership.

TURNOVER BY CATEGORY
---------------------
Category              Q1 2025     Q4 2024     Change
                     (Annual.)   (Annual.)
Physicians            18.2%       12.1%       +50.4%
Registered Nurses     19.8%       15.4%       +28.6%
Allied Health         24.1%       18.6%       +29.6%
Administrative        28.3%       21.2%       +33.5%
Support Staff         26.7%       20.8%       +28.4%
Overall               22.4%       16.8%       +33.3%

DEPARTURES IN Q1 2025
----------------------
Total staff departures: 127 (out of approximately 2,265 total FTEs)
- Voluntary resignations: 98 (77.2%)
- Involuntary terminations: 12 (9.4%)
- Retirements: 11 (8.7%)
- Contract expirations: 6 (4.7%)

TOP REASONS FOR VOLUNTARY DEPARTURE (EXIT SURVEY DATA)
-------------------------------------------------------
1. Burnout / workload (34% of exit survey respondents)
2. Better compensation elsewhere (28%)
3. Limited career advancement (16%)
4. Poor work-life balance (12%)
5. Management issues (7%)
6. Relocation (3%)

CRITICAL DEPARTURES
--------------------
The following high-impact departures occurred in Q1:
- 3 Emergency Department physicians (cited burnout)
- 2 Primary care physicians
- 2 Medical specialists (cardiology, neurology)
- 1 Department director (Radiology)
- 5 senior RNs with 10+ years experience
- 2 IT infrastructure specialists (during system migration)

COST OF TURNOVER
-----------------
Estimated cost of Q1 turnover: $3.8M
- Physician replacement cost: ~$250K per physician (recruitment, credentialing,
  lost revenue during vacancy)
- Nurse replacement cost: ~$45K per nurse
- Administrative/support: ~$15K per position
- Temporary staffing costs to cover vacancies: $1.8M

IMPACT ON OPERATIONS
---------------------
High turnover is creating cascading effects:
- Remaining staff facing increased workload → more burnout → more departures
- Quality of care concerns in departments with high vacancy rates
- Training burden on existing staff to orient new hires
- Institutional knowledge loss, especially with senior departures

NOTE ON NURSING DEPARTMENT DISCREPANCY
-----------------------------------------
While the Nursing Department report indicates net positive hiring (+27 FTEs),
it should be noted that many new hires are recent graduates or transfers from
other facilities who require significant orientation and mentoring. The loss of
experienced nurses (average 8.5 years tenure) versus new hires (average 1.2
years experience) represents a meaningful reduction in overall nursing expertise,
despite the improved headcount numbers.

Additionally, the nursing engagement survey's positive results may not fully
capture the sentiment of nurses who have already left. Exit survey data paints
a different picture, with 34% citing burnout as their primary reason.

RECOMMENDATIONS
---------------
1. Implement retention bonuses for high-risk departments (ED, ICU)
2. Expand wellness and burnout prevention programs
3. Conduct competitive compensation analysis and adjust
4. Create career pathway programs in all departments
5. Reduce reliance on overtime through strategic hiring
6. Monthly turnover dashboards to department heads
