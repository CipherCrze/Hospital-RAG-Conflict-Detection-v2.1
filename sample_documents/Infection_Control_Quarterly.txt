===============================================
   CITYCARE GENERAL HOSPITAL
   Infection Prevention & Control — Quarterly Report
   Period: January – March 2025
===============================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
The Infection Prevention & Control (IPC) Department reports mixed results for
Q1 2025. Hospital-wide infection rates showed an overall decrease, but certain
categories of healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) showed concerning
increases that warrant attention.

HOSPITAL-WIDE INFECTION METRICS
---------------------------------
Overall HAI rate: 2.8 per 1,000 patient-days (Q4 2024: 3.1)
- This represents a 9.7% decrease in overall infection rate.
- The hospital remains below the national benchmark of 3.2 per 1,000
  patient-days.

HAI BREAKDOWN BY TYPE
----------------------
Infection Type                         Q1 2025   Q4 2024   Change
Central Line-Associated BSI (CLABSI)   0.4       0.6       -33.3%
Catheter-Associated UTI (CAUTI)        0.8       1.0       -20.0%
Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (VAP)  0.3       0.4       -25.0%
Surgical Site Infections (SSI)         1.0       0.8       +25.0%
C. difficile Infections                0.3       0.3        0.0%

HAND HYGIENE COMPLIANCE
-------------------------
- Overall hand hygiene compliance rate: 89% (up from 82% in Q4)
- By role:
  - Physicians: 84% (up from 74%)
  - Nurses: 93% (up from 88%)
  - Allied Health: 87% (up from 79%)
  - Support Staff: 85% (up from 76%)

SURGICAL SITE INFECTION CONCERN
---------------------------------
While most HAI categories improved, surgical site infections increased by
25%. This is consistent with the concerns raised by Dr. Lisa Chang in her
separate memo regarding post-operative complications. Contributing factors
include:
- Increased surgical volume without proportional staffing
- Equipment sterilization issues in February
- Higher proportion of emergency procedures

The IPC team is working closely with the Surgical Department to implement
enhanced SSI prevention protocols including:
- Pre-operative antibiotic timing audits
- Surgical skin preparation standardization
- Post-operative wound care education
- Environmental cleaning audits of operating rooms

POSITIVE DEVELOPMENTS
----------------------
1. Successful implementation of antimicrobial stewardship program — antibiotic
   usage reduced by 12% with no increase in treatment failures
2. Installation of UV-C disinfection robots in ICU and OR suites
3. Achievement of 100% influenza vaccination among clinical staff
4. Zero healthcare worker needlestick injuries in March

ENVIRONMENTAL SERVICES
-----------------------
- Terminal cleaning compliance: 95% (up from 88%)
- High-touch surface cleaning scores improved across all units
- New automated hand sanitizer dispensers installed (142 units)
