===============================================
   CITYCARE GENERAL HOSPITAL
   Nursing Department — Q1 Staff Report
   Period: January – March 2025
===============================================

STAFFING OVERVIEW
-----------------
The Nursing Department made significant progress in Q1 2025 toward our
staffing improvement goals. Thanks to an aggressive recruitment campaign
launched in November 2024, we have successfully improved our nurse-to-patient
ratios across most departments.

NURSE-TO-PATIENT RATIOS
-------------------------
Department              Q1 2025     Q4 2024     Target
Medical/Surgical        1:5         1:7         1:4
ICU                     1:2         1:2         1:2
Pediatrics              1:4         1:5         1:3
Emergency Department    1:6         1:4         1:4
Labor & Delivery        1:2         1:3         1:2

Overall hospital nurse-to-patient ratio improved from 1:5.4 to 1:4.8.

NEW HIRES & RETENTION
----------------------
- New RN hires in Q1: 45
- RN departures in Q1: 18
- Net nursing staff gain: +27
- Current total nursing FTEs: 412 (up from 385 in Q4)
- Nursing vacancy rate: 8.2% (down from 14.1% in Q4)

STAFF MORALE & ENGAGEMENT
--------------------------
The annual Nursing Staff Engagement Survey conducted in February 2025 showed
notable improvements:
- Overall engagement score: 78% (up from 64% in 2024)
- "I feel valued by my employer": 72% agree (up from 55%)
- "I have adequate resources to do my job well": 68% agree (up from 51%)
- "I would recommend CityCarе as a workplace": 75% agree (up from 58%)
- "I plan to stay at CityCarе for at least 2 more years": 71% agree

Staff morale has improved substantially, attributed to:
1. Competitive salary adjustments (average 8% raise effective January 2025)
2. New flexible scheduling options
3. Expansion of mental health support services
4. Leadership recognition programs

PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
-------------------------
- 89 nurses completed advanced certifications in Q1
- New nurse residency program retention rate: 94%
- 12 nurses enrolled in BSN-to-MSN programs with hospital tuition support

CHALLENGES
----------
- Emergency Department nursing remains critically understaffed despite
  overall improvements elsewhere. ED nurse-to-patient ratio worsened to 1:6,
  below target of 1:4.
- Overtime dependency remains high in the ED: 35% of ED nursing hours
  were overtime in Q1.
- Agency/travel nurse costs in ED: $1.2M in Q1 (42% over budget)
