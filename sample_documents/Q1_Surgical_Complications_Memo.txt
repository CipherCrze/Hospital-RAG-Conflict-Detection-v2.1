Dear Dr. Howard and Dr. Santos,

I am writing to bring to your attention a concerning trend in post-operative
complications that I believe warrants immediate review.

POST-OPERATIVE INFECTION DATA (Q1 2025)
-----------------------------------------
While the Surgical Department's overall success rate remains high, our
post-operative infection tracking reveals a troubling upward trend:

- Total post-op infections: 78 cases (out of 1,845 procedures)
- Post-operative infection rate: 4.2%
- Q4 2024 post-op infection rate: 3.9%
- Net increase: +8% rise in post-op infection rate

Breakdown by infection type:
- Surgical Site Infections (SSI): 26 cases (1.4%)
- Urinary Tract Infections (catheter-associated): 22 cases (1.2%)
- Pneumonia (ventilator-associated): 18 cases (1.0%)
- Bloodstream Infections (central line-associated): 12 cases (0.7%)

COMPARISON WITH NATIONAL BENCHMARKS
-------------------------------------
While our SSI rate of 1.4% is within national benchmarks, the OVERALL
post-operative infection rate of 4.2% exceeds the national average of 3.5%.
More concerning is the upward trajectory:
- Q3 2024: 3.2%
- Q4 2024: 3.9%
- Q1 2025: 4.2%

This represents a 31% increase over the past two quarters.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (PRELIMINARY)
-----------------------------------
Our initial investigation suggests several contributing factors:
1. Increased surgical volume (+15%) without proportional increase in
   sterile processing staff
2. Two autoclaves were out of service for repairs in February
3. Increased use of temporary/agency surgical nurses who may not be
   fully oriented to our sterilization protocols
4. Higher proportion of emergency surgeries (which carry inherently
   higher infection risk)

PATIENT IMPACT
--------------
- 78 patients experienced post-operative infections
- Average additional hospital stay: 4.8 days per infected patient
- 3 patients required ICU admission due to sepsis
- 1 mortality potentially related to post-operative infection
  (under review)
- Estimated additional cost to hospital: $890,000

DISCREPANCY NOTE
-----------------
I note that the Surgical Outcomes Report (prepared by Dr. Williams) focuses
primarily on SSI rates (1.4%) and the overall "success rate" of 96.2%.
While these figures are accurate, they do not fully capture the broader
post-operative infection picture. I recommend that future reports include
ALL post-operative infection categories for a more complete view.

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS
---------------------
1. Immediate audit of sterile processing procedures
2. Mandatory re-orientation for all agency surgical staff
3. Expand post-operative infection tracking to include all categories
4. Convene Infection Control Committee emergency meeting
5. Consider temporary reduction in elective surgical volume until
   infection rates stabilize

I request a meeting to discuss these findings at your earliest convenience.

Respectfully,
Dr. Lisa Chang
Infection Control Officer
