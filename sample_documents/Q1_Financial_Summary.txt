===============================================
   CITYCARE GENERAL HOSPITAL
   Finance Department — Q1 Financial Summary
   Period: January – March 2025
===============================================

REVENUE HIGHLIGHTS
------------------
Total Revenue:        $48.2M  (Q4 2024: $43.0M,  +12.1%)
Net Patient Revenue:  $42.8M  (Q4 2024: $38.1M,  +12.3%)
Other Revenue:        $5.4M   (Q4 2024: $4.9M,   +10.2%)

Revenue growth was driven by:
- Increased surgical volume (+15% procedures)
- Higher inpatient admissions (+9%)
- New telehealth billing codes generating $1.2M
- Imaging center expansion revenue: $0.8M

EXPENSE OVERVIEW
-----------------
Total Operating Expenses: $44.9M  (Q4 2024: $40.2M,  +11.7%)

Expense breakdown:
- Salaries & Benefits:    $28.1M  (62.6% of expenses, up 14.2%)
- Supplies & Equipment:   $7.8M   (17.4%, up 8.3%)
- Facility & Maintenance: $3.2M   (7.1%, up 5.6%)
- IT & Technology:        $2.4M   (5.3%, up 22.4%)
- Agency/Contract Staff:  $1.8M   (4.0%, up 45.2%)
- Other:                  $1.6M   (3.6%, up 3.9%)

KEY FINANCIAL METRICS
---------------------
- Operating Income:    $3.3M (margin: 6.8%, above industry average of 4.2%)
- Days Cash on Hand:   145 days (strong liquidity position)
- Accounts Receivable: $12.4M (days in A/R: 47, target: <45)
- Bad Debt:            $1.1M (2.3% of net revenue, stable)

The hospital's overall financial position remains strong with healthy revenue
growth outpacing expense increases.

AREAS OF CONCERN
-----------------
1. Agency staffing costs increased 45.2% to $1.8M, primarily driven by
   ED staffing needs. This is unsustainable long-term.
2. IT costs surged 22.4% due to unplanned system repairs and the scheduling
   platform migration.
3. Salary increases (8% average nursing raise) contributed to the 14.2%
   increase in personnel costs.
4. Supply chain disruptions caused a 12% price increase in surgical supplies.

CAPITAL EXPENDITURES
---------------------
Q1 capital spending: $2.8M
- New imaging equipment: $1.2M
- Facilities renovation: $0.9M
- IT infrastructure: $0.5M
- Medical equipment: $0.2M

Approved but not yet spent:
- New imaging center construction: $8.5M (breaking ground Q2)
- EHR upgrade completion: $1.5M

CASH FLOW
----------
The hospital generated $4.1M in operating cash flow during Q1, sufficient
to fund capital expenditures and maintain reserve levels. However, the
planned imaging center construction will require the approved $15M credit
facility starting in Q2.
