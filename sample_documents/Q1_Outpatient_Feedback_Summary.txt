===============================================
   CITYCARE GENERAL HOSPITAL
   Outpatient Services — Q1 Feedback Summary
   Period: January – March 2025
===============================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Outpatient services at CityCarе General Hospital experienced a notable decline
in patient satisfaction during Q1 2025. Despite improvements reported in
inpatient surveys, the outpatient experience has deteriorated across multiple
dimensions.

SATISFACTION SCORES
-------------------
Overall outpatient satisfaction: 64.3%
Q4 2024 outpatient satisfaction: 73.1%
Change: -12.0% decrease (dropped 8.8 percentage points)

BREAKDOWN BY SERVICE AREA
--------------------------
Service Area              Q1 2025   Q4 2024   Change
Primary Care Clinics      62%       71%       -12.7%
Specialty Clinics         68%       76%       -10.5%
Diagnostic Imaging        59%       72%       -18.1%
Laboratory Services       71%       74%       -4.1%
Physical Therapy          75%       77%       -2.6%
Pharmacy                  66%       73%       -9.6%

MAJOR ISSUES IDENTIFIED
-------------------------
1. APPOINTMENT ACCESS (Critical)
   - Average wait for new patient appointment: 28 days (up from 16 days)
   - Average wait for follow-up appointment: 14 days (up from 8 days)
   - 23% of patients reported being unable to get an appointment within
     a reasonable timeframe
   - Online scheduling system experienced 12 outages in Q1

2. IN-CLINIC WAIT TIMES
   - Average wait past appointment time: 47 minutes (up from 28 minutes)
   - 34% of patients waited more than 1 hour past their appointment time
   - Diagnostic imaging wait times doubled due to equipment issues

3. COMMUNICATION
   - 31% of outpatients reported difficulty reaching their provider's office
   - Average callback time: 3.2 business days (target: 1 business day)
   - Test result notification delays averaging 5.8 days (target: 3 days)

4. BILLING
   - Billing error rate: 8.4% (up from 5.2%)
   - 156 patients reported receiving incorrect bills
   - Average time to resolve billing disputes: 22 days

PATIENT VERBATIM FEEDBACK (REPRESENTATIVE)
--------------------------------------------
- "I love my doctor, but it's impossible to get an appointment. I had to
  wait 5 weeks for a routine follow-up."
- "The online scheduling system never works. I've tried booking online
  three times and had to call each time."
- "I sat in the waiting room for over 2 hours. No one apologized or
  explained the delay."
- "I was billed $600 for a test my insurance should have covered. It took
  a month to get it corrected."

IMPACT ON HOSPITAL METRICS
----------------------------
- Patient leakage: Estimated 340 patients sought care elsewhere in Q1 due
  to access issues (up from 180 in Q4)
- Revenue impact: Estimated $1.8M in lost outpatient revenue
- Reputation risk: 42 negative online reviews mentioning outpatient 
  experience (up from 15 in Q4)

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS
---------------------
- Loss of 3 primary care physicians and 2 specialists in Q4 2024
  (not yet replaced)
- IT system migration caused scheduling disruptions in January
- Radiology equipment (CT scanner and one MRI) out of service for
  6 weeks for repairs
- Insufficient front desk staffing to handle call volume

RECOMMENDATIONS
---------------
1. Emergency recruitment of primary care and specialist physicians
2. Investment in reliable online scheduling platform
3. Hire 8 additional front desk/scheduling staff
4. Implement patient communication improvements (automated updates)
5. Conduct billing process audit and staff retraining

Prepared by: Ms. Jennifer Walsh, Outpatient Services Director
