   CITYCARE GENERAL HOSPITAL
   Finance Department — Q1 Budget Variance Report
   Period: January – March 2025
===============================================

SUMMARY
-------
Despite strong revenue performance in Q1 2025, significant budget overruns
were observed across multiple departments. Total expenses exceeded the
approved budget by $2.7M (6.4% over budget), raising concerns about fiscal
discipline and long-term sustainability.

MAJOR BUDGET OVERRUNS
----------------------
1. EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT (+$1.3M, +22.4% over budget)
   - Primary driver: Agency staffing costs ($1.2M vs $0.6M budgeted)
   - Secondary: Overtime expenses ($0.4M vs $0.2M budgeted)
   - The ED budget was set based on assumptions of full staffing by February,
     which did not materialize due to physician resignations.

2. IT/TECHNOLOGY (+$0.6M, +33.3% over budget)
   - Unplanned scheduling system migration: $0.3M
   - Equipment repairs and replacements: $0.2M
   - Cybersecurity incident response costs: $0.1M

3. NURSING (+$0.7M, +8.5% over budget)
   - Salary adjustments (8% raise) not fully captured in original budget
   - Agency nurse costs in ED allocated partially to Nursing budget
   - New hire orientation and training costs

4. SURGERY (+$0.4M, +6.3% over budget)
   - Supply chain price increases (+12% on surgical supplies)
   - Increased volume driving supply costs higher

CONFLICT WITH FINANCIAL SUMMARY
---------------------------------
While the Q1 Financial Summary presents a positive picture with 6.8%
operating margin, it is important to note:
- The positive margin was achieved DESPITE these overruns, primarily due
  to better-than-expected revenue
- If revenue had grown at the budgeted rate (8%) instead of the actual
  rate (12.1%), the hospital would have operated at a 1.2% margin — barely
  above breakeven
- The expense overruns represent structural issues (staffing, IT) that will
  likely persist or worsen in Q2 unless addressed

PROJECTED IMPACT ON ANNUAL BUDGET
-----------------------------------
If Q1 trends continue:
- Projected annual budget overrun: $8.4M–$11.2M
- This would reduce the annual operating margin from the planned 5.5% to
  an estimated 2.8%–3.5%
- Capital expenditure plans (imaging center) may need to be reconsidered

RECOMMENDATIONS
---------------
1. Immediate hiring freeze on non-critical positions
2. Mandatory department head budget review meetings (monthly)
3. Renegotiate agency staffing contracts
4. Defer non-essential IT projects to Q3/Q4
5. Revise annual budget to reflect actual cost trends
6. Consider phasing the imaging center construction
